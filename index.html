History & Identity

Ancient Roots +

The land of Palestine is steeped in history, home to some of the world's oldest civilizations. Its strategic location at the crossroads of Asia, Africa, and Europe made it a center for trade and a melting pot of cultures. Archaeological findings date human presence back hundreds of thousands of years.

The Ottoman Era +

For over four centuries, from 1516 to 1917, Palestine was part of the Ottoman Empire. During this period, the region was diverse, with a population of Muslims, Christians, and Jews living in a complex and multi-religious society. The period saw a relative stability and a degree of autonomy for local populations.

The British Mandate & Beyond +

After World War I, the League of Nations granted the United Kingdom a mandate over Palestine. This period saw significant political and demographic changes, leading to rising tensions between Arab and Jewish populations. The events that followed shaped the modern history of the region.

Culture & Art

The Keffiyeh

The iconic black and white scarf, a powerful symbol of Palestinian nationalism and identity, worn by people worldwide.

Palestinian Cuisine

A vibrant and rich culinary tradition with dishes like falafel, hummus, and the famous Maqluba, a delicious upside-down rice and meat dish.

Tatreez Embroidery

The intricate, colorful patterns of Tatreez tell stories of village life, family, and history, passed down through generations.
